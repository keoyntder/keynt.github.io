Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet capable of sustaining life. It has a diverse structure, consisting of a solid crust, a molten mantle, and a dense core, with about 71% of its surface covered by water.Surrounded by an atmosphere rich in oxygen and nitrogen, it supports a wide variety of ecosystems and organisms. Earth orbits the Sun at an average distance of 93 million miles, completing a rotation every 24 hours and a revolution every 365.25 days.

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in our solar system, known for its massive size and striking appearance. It is a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface and a turbulent atmosphere marked by colorful bands and the iconic Great Red Spot. Jupiter has a strong magnetic field and at least 95 moons, including the four largest, known as the Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. Orbiting the Sun at an average distance of 484 million miles, it completes a rotation in just 10 hours but takes nearly 12 Earth years to complete one orbit.
